## Design Context

### Users

Lofiever serves people who keep a shared lo-fi radio playing while they study, work, create, or unwind. Listening is the primary job; chat is a lightweight, communal way to influence the station without turning the experience into a music-production tool. The same synchronized broadcast is heard by everyone, on desktop, mobile, and TV.

### Brand Personality

Editorial, tactile, and quietly playful. Lofiever should feel like a small independent radio station with a living point of view: calm enough for focus, human enough to invite participation, and opinionated enough to remain a curated broadcast rather than a generic jukebox.

### Aesthetic Direction

The approved direction is a “living radio-zine”: risograph-inspired artwork per track, editorial typography, edition-based palettes, light/night variants, and an immersive Zen mode. New features should extend the existing `BroadcastApp` language and preserve the public experience's decision not to display listener counts. Avoid generic AI dashboards, neon-on-dark “AI” styling, glassmorphism, nested cards, and workflow-heavy production interfaces.

### Design Principles

1. Keep listening primary; creation happens through natural conversation with Lofine.
2. Make asynchronous work legible with honest states and calm, specific copy—never fake progress.
3. Treat generated music as part of the station's editorial identity, with transparent AI provenance and no claims of human authorship or exclusivity.
4. Preserve the shared-broadcast feeling: requested tracks join the communal program instead of becoming private playback.
5. Use the existing radio-zine vocabulary, responsive behavior, keyboard access, reduced-motion support, and PT/EN localization.
